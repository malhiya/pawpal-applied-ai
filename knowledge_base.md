Medication tasks are NON_NEGOTIABLE — missed doses can be dangerous to the pet's health.
Vet appointments are NON_NEGOTIABLE — they are scheduled medical commitments that cannot be skipped.
Health-related tasks override all other scheduling considerations.
Feeding times are HIGH priority — consistent meal times are critical for digestive health and behavior.
Dog walks are HIGH priority — dogs require daily physical activity for health and behavior.
Grooming tasks are MEDIUM priority — important for hygiene but can be rescheduled if needed.
Playtime and enrichment are LOW priority — beneficial but the most flexible tasks in the schedule.
Training sessions are LOW priority — helpful but optional and flexible.
Medication must be given daily at the same consistent time — frequency is daily.
Feeding should occur at consistent times every day — frequency is daily.
Dog walks should happen every day — frequency is daily.
Weekly grooming sessions repeat once per week — frequency is weekly.
Vet appointments and one-time events have the same start date and end date.
Medication duration is typically 5 minutes.
Feeding a meal duration is typically 10 minutes.
Short walk duration is typically 20 minutes.
Standard walk duration is typically 30 minutes.
Vet appointment duration is typically 60 minutes.
Grooming session duration is typically 20 minutes.
Bath duration is typically 45 minutes.
Nail trimming duration is typically 15 minutes.
Playtime duration is typically 20 minutes.
Training session duration is typically 15 minutes.
Morning tasks default to scheduled time 08:00.
After breakfast tasks default to scheduled time 08:30.
Afternoon tasks default to scheduled time 14:00.
Evening tasks or after dinner tasks default to scheduled time 18:00.
Night or bedtime tasks default to scheduled time 21:00.
Lunchtime tasks default to scheduled time 12:00.
Next weekday means the upcoming occurrence of that day after today — it is a one-time event.
Two tasks cannot overlap in scheduled time on the same day — conflicting tasks must be adjusted by changing time or duration.
If a task conflicts with an existing task, it should be skipped and the user should be notified to adjust the time.
Dogs should be groomed every 4 to 8 weeks — frequency depends on breed and coat length.
Long-haired dogs require brushing several times a week to prevent matting and tangles.
Short-haired dogs require brushing once a week to remove loose fur and keep the coat healthy.
Dogs should be bathed every 4 to 8 weeks unless they get dirty or have a skin condition.
Dog nails should be trimmed every 3 to 4 weeks to prevent overgrowth and discomfort.
Cats generally groom themselves but long-haired cats benefit from brushing every 1 to 2 days.
Short-haired cats should be brushed once a week to reduce shedding and hairballs.
Cats rarely need baths unless medically necessary or they cannot groom themselves.
Cat nails should be trimmed every 2 to 3 weeks to prevent scratching and overgrowth.
Regular ear cleaning and dental care are part of grooming for both dogs and cats.
